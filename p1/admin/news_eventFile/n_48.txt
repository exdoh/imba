<p><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="font-family: Arial"><span style="line-height: 115%">After months of waiting and preparation, 29 IMBA GE#3 students finally got on board for their trip to Stanford University and the Green MBA Program at Dominican University, San Rafael. The excitement started right then; the young future entrepreneurs were ready to experience the other perspectives and explore for new opportunities to bring back home. All the 29 lives went there three days before the academic journey started to tour around San Francisco. &ldquo;After long waiting time for the trip, we are finally going&rdquo; said Kittitat Montrechok.</span></span></span></p>
<div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="font-family: Arial"><span style="line-height: 115%">While enjoying the city tour, the young energetic crowd never forgot the purpose of their trip. After their city tour during the day; at night, GE#3 students gathered into groups to work on their water project, which challenged the class to encourage entrepreneurs in Thailand to solve problems related to water issues. </span></span></span></div>
<div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="font-family: Arial"><span style="line-height: 115%">On the third day, students packed their luggages and headed for Stanford University. They were to join 5-days panel discussions and lectures organized during the Asia-Pacific Student Entrepreneurship Society (ASES) summit. At the conference, IMBA students had opportunities to learn, explore, experience, and be inspired by entrepreneurs and experts on various topics such as clean technologies, entrepreneurial experiences, and social enterprises. The program also organized field trips to the area businesses and one of the highlights was the visit to IDEO, Palo Alto-base renowned design house. </span></span></span></div>
<div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="font-family: Arial"><span style="line-height: 115%">Not only were GE #3 was there to attend lectures, the class also served their duty as Thai cultural ambassador. The group hosted the &ldquo;Songkran&rdquo; dinner to share the warm Thai culture and hospitality. It was first time that the young ASES&rsquo;s enjoyed the fun of Thai tradition.</span></span></span></div>
<div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="font-family: Arial"><span style="line-height: 115%">On the second week of the trip, IMBA students headed to the &ldquo;Green MBA&rdquo; program at the Dominican University of California at San Rafael. The program organized various workshops for the class. The workshop started in the afternoon right after the arrival; it was the &ldquo;conversation mapping&rdquo; workshop. &ldquo;I think it&rsquo;s a very useful tool since it allows the team to communicate and extend our creativity beyond the boundary&rdquo; quoted Kulthida Wiratkapan. For the rest of the week, the program organized lectures and trips to visit various organizations to light up and inspire the class with the ideas for their water projects. The trips include visit to the Sonoma County Water Agency, Elkon Slough, and the Sonoma County Wineries were designed to show water sustainability concepts and practices, as well as to inspire how these green concepts can be done at home. </span></span></span></div>
<div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="font-family: Arial"><span style="line-height: 115%">On the last day at the Green MBA program, the GE#3 class presented their ideas and progresses of their water projects to the Green MBA faculty and fellow students. The academic trip came to an end as the class showed their appreciation in a traditional Thai way; &ldquo;Rod Nam Dam Hua&rdquo; the Green MBA faculty. So, not only the trip was about academic and fun, but it was an opportunity to show the world about Thailand as well.</span></span></span></div>
