<p><span style="line-height: 115%">Even with the rise of China and India, Japan continues to the economic leader of Asia, and it is the land of rich traditional culture mixed with modern influences. Some of us had been to Japan before, but all of us were equally excited. We landed on Saturday morning and were greeted by Ms. Keiko Uchiyama, our English speaking tour guide for this trip. We started our day with the Edo Tokyo Museum, Aikihabara, and Odaiba Fuji TV where we received small prizes for completing the Fuji TV stamp hunting.&nbsp;Contest later, we arrived at our hotel in Tachikawa. The following day, we went to the Imperial Plaza Palace, Asakusa and Meiji Shrine where we wrote our prayers on wooden pedal and hung it under the tree. </span></p>
<div style="text-justify: inter-cluster; margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">During the weekdays at Hitotsubashi University, we had various classes relating to how to do business in Japan such as Japanese Business Culture, Japanese Customer Behavior, and Japanese Business History. We also looked at where Japan is in terms of the global economy through classes such as International Finance and Global Economy. At the end of the week, we had a discussion session with Hitotsubashi MBA student about how to market King Rice Bran Oil in Japan, which is one of our independent study topics.</span></div>
<div style="text-justify: inter-cluster; margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">Apart from lectures within the classroom, we had an opportunity to gain deeper insider knowledge from Executive Director of Thai Trade Center in Tokyo at the Office of Commercial Affairs Royal Thai Embassy. Afterwards, we went to see Meji Seika Kanto Factory and the production of Meiji snacks and chocolate. The next day, we arrived at Sony headquarters. At Sony, we were very fortunate to see The SQUARE, Sony&rsquo;s exclusive showroom. We later visited Ryohinkeikaku (Muji) Yurakucho store and headquarter at Higashi-Ikebukuro. We were welcomed and received a lecture from Mr. Kei Suzuki, the Executive Officer and General Manager of Ryohin Keikaju Co., Ltd.</span></div>
<div style="text-justify: inter-cluster; margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">On the lighter side of our visit, we had Japanese Language Workshop which we were divided into groups to go interview Hitotsubashi students about various topics in Japanese. This is where we showed how much Japanese we have learned. We had an opportunity to see and participate in the Japanese Tea Ceremony with Professor Akiba and a demonstration of the ceremony from two Thai students. </span></div>
<div style="text-justify: inter-cluster; margin: 0in 0in 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">Even though, the trip had come to an end, this valuable experience will always be memorable for all of us for many years to come.</span></div>
