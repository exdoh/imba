<p><span style="line-height: 115%">The International MBA Program (IMBA) of the Thammasat Business School has been invited by the Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley, to be a partner of the Global Social Venture Competition (GSVC) for 2008-2009. GSVC </span><span style="line-height: 115%">was launched in 1999 by the Haas School of Business, and was the first graduate-student business plan competition to promote entrepreneurial start-up companies that offer measurable social or environmental benefits, in addition to profits. By 2008 GSVC has grown to include over 250 teams worldwide, partnering with many of the world&rsquo;s top business schools, including the Columbia Business School, the London Business School, the Yale School of Management, the Indian School of Business, and now the Thammasat Business School.</span></p>
<div style="margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">The GSVC is based on an innovative method of business valuation, called the triple bottom-line. &nbsp;It promotes the creation of financially profitable social ventures, by educating and connecting students, entrepreneurs, investors. &nbsp;Several ventures that passed through GSVC have gone on to became successful businesses in the US and around the world, including Ethos Water, which was bought out by Starbucks, Sweetriot, and World of Good.</span></div>
<div style="margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">As one of the GSVC partners, the Thammasat Business School hosts the GSVC in Southeast Asia, which seeks the most promising business ideas in the region.&nbsp;Winners of the GSVC &ndash; Southeast Asia, win prizes totally USD 10,000 and represent the region at the Global Final round at UC Berkeley. &nbsp;The competition is open for graduate students enrolled in any university from Thailand and Southeast Asian countries. Students who are interested in the Competition can enter the GSVC by submitting a five-page executive summary of a proposed venture, which is scalable and offers quantifiable social and/or environmental benefits, to <a href="mailto:gsvc.sea@gmail.com">gsvc.sea@gmail.com</a> from now until 9 January 2009. Selected teams will be invited to present their business plans at the GSVC&mdash;SEA Presentation Round at the Thammasat Business School, in Bangkok on 19-21 March 2009, where they will be judged by successful social entrepreneurs, investors, business people from around Southeast Asia and the rest of the world. </span></div>
<div style="margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt"><span style="line-height: 115%">For more information about GSVC competition, please visit <a target="_blank" href="http://www.gsvc-sea.org.">www.gsvc-sea.org.</a></span></div>
